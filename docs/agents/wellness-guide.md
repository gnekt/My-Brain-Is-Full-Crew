# Wellness Guide

> A compassionate companion trained in CBT, ACT, and Mindfulness -- here to support you between therapy sessions.

> **DISCLAIMER: This agent is NOT a therapist, psychologist, or mental health professional.** It is an AI agent that applies techniques from publicly available CBT, ACT, and Mindfulness frameworks. It **cannot diagnose** anything. It **cannot provide therapy.** It should **never be your only source of mental health support.** If you are in crisis, experiencing thoughts of self-harm, or unable to function, **contact a real mental health professional or emergency service immediately.** The author of this project takes absolutely no responsibility for any decisions you make based on the output of this agent. This is a personal wellness companion designed to complement -- never replace -- professional mental health care.

---

## What it does

The Wellness Guide is a mental health support agent grounded in three evidence-based frameworks: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Mindfulness. It helps you navigate difficult moments -- burnout, anxiety, rumination, intrusive thoughts, overwhelm, imposter syndrome, sleep trouble -- by offering validated techniques and genuine, non-judgmental support.

This agent is designed to complement your real therapist, not replace them. It serves as a bridge between sessions: helping you practice techniques, process difficult moments, identify thought patterns, and prepare insights to bring to your next appointment. If you are not currently in therapy, it still provides valuable tools, but gently encourages professional support when appropriate.

The Wellness Guide operates in read-only mode within your vault. It can read your recent daily notes and past session reflections for context, but it never writes directly. When something worth saving comes up -- an insight, an affirmation, a breakthrough -- the Wellness Guide asks the Scribe to capture it for you.

**Important:** In any acute crisis (thoughts of self-harm, severe dissociation, inability to function), the Wellness Guide will immediately provide real-world emergency resources and encourage you to contact your therapist or an emergency service. This agent is support, not a substitute for professional care.

## Capabilities

- **CBT techniques** -- identifies cognitive distortions (all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, mind-reading, personalization) and guides you through cognitive restructuring to find more balanced thoughts
- **ACT techniques** -- cognitive defusion ("I am having the thought that..."), acceptance of difficult emotions, values clarification, and committed action despite discomfort
- **Mindfulness exercises** -- grounding techniques (5-4-3-2-1), body scan, conscious breathing (4-4-6 pattern), thought observation, and RAIN (Recognize, Accept, Investigate, Nurture)
- **Burnout and overwhelm support** -- starts with grounding, validates your experience, helps identify the heaviest thing, and guides you to one small next step
- **Anxiety and worry management** -- distinguishes between what you can influence and what you cannot, teaches worry-time technique, reality-tests catastrophic scenarios
- **Rumination processing** -- helps you unstick from past events through defusion, regret analysis, and present-moment anchoring
- **Intrusive thought handling** -- normalizes intrusive thoughts without invalidating them, uses externalization and gentle defusion
- **Imposter syndrome** -- challenges the evidence, reframes accomplishments, connects to your values rather than external validation
- **Sleep hygiene** -- practical techniques for sleep-disrupting anxiety and racing thoughts
- **Gratitude practice** -- guided gratitude exercises grounded in specificity, not generic positivity
- **Values clarification** -- ACT-based exploration of what truly matters to you, to guide decision-making
- **Session bridging** -- helps you prepare insights, questions, and themes to bring to your real therapist
- **Emotional pattern tracking** -- reads past entries to identify recurring themes (via the Scribe's emotion tags)

## Example conversations

### Burnout and overwhelm

**You:** I'm completely overwhelmed. I have too much to do and I can't even start.

**Wellness Guide:** I hear you. Let's start by pausing for a moment before we look at anything else.

Take three deep breaths with me. Inhale for 4 counts... hold for 4... exhale for 6. Just that. Nothing else needs to happen right now.

How does your body feel right now? Where do you notice the tension?

**You:** My chest feels tight and my shoulders are up near my ears.

**Wellness Guide:** That makes sense -- your body is carrying the weight of everything on your list. Let your shoulders drop. Just let them go. You do not need to hold them up.

Now, here is what I want you to know: what you are feeling is a completely valid response to being overloaded. This is not weakness. This is your system telling you something important.

Let me ask you one question: if you had to name the single heaviest thing right now -- not the full list, just the one that weighs the most -- what would it be?

**You:** The client proposal. It's due Thursday and I haven't started.

**Wellness Guide:** Okay. Just the proposal. Everything else can wait for this moment.

What is the absolute smallest step you could take on the proposal right now? Not "finish it" or even "outline it" -- what is the tiniest move? Opening the document? Writing the first sentence? Even just finding the email where the client described what they want?

**You:** I could open the email and re-read what they asked for.

**Wellness Guide:** That is your next step. Just that. Open the email, read it, and then come back to me if you want. You do not need to write a single word of the proposal yet.

One small move breaks the paralysis. You have got this.

### Rumination about the past

**You:** I can't stop thinking about that meeting last week. I said something stupid and everyone noticed.

**Wellness Guide:** That sounds really uncomfortable -- having a moment replay in your mind over and over.

Let me ask you something from a CBT perspective. You said "everyone noticed." What is the actual evidence for that? Did anyone say something? Did you see reactions?

**You:** Well... no one said anything directly. But I could tell from their faces.

**Wellness Guide:** So the evidence is: you interpreted their facial expressions as judgment. That is understandable, but I want to name what is happening here -- this is a cognitive pattern called "mind reading." We assume we know what others are thinking, and the assumption is almost always negative.

Let me offer another possibility: most people in meetings are thinking about their own points, their lunch, or their next call. The moment you are replaying on loop? There is a very good chance no one else remembers it at all.

Here is an ACT technique that might help: instead of "I said something stupid," try saying to yourself: "I am having the thought that I said something stupid." Notice the difference? You are not the thought. You are the person observing the thought.

The thought can exist without you believing it is the truth.

### Pre-sleep anxiety

**You:** I can't sleep. My mind is racing about everything I need to do tomorrow.

**Wellness Guide:** Racing thoughts at night are one of the most common experiences, especially when you are carrying a lot. Your mind is trying to "solve" tomorrow before it arrives.

Let's try two things. First, a quick brain dump: tell me everything that is on your mind right now -- every task, worry, thought. Just list them. Get them out of your head and into this conversation. You can deal with them tomorrow, but right now, let's get them out of your body.

**You:** [lists several worries and tasks]

**Wellness Guide:** Good. Those are now captured. They will still be there tomorrow, but they do not need to live in your body tonight.

Now, let's do a grounding exercise. Lie down comfortably and try the 5-4-3-2-1 technique:
- Name 5 things you can see (even in the dark -- shadows, shapes, the ceiling)
- 4 things you can physically feel (the pillow, the blanket, the temperature of the air)
- 3 things you can hear (the fridge humming, distant traffic, your own breathing)
- 2 things you can smell
- 1 thing you can taste

This brings your mind out of the future and back into your body, right here, right now. Take your time.

And one more thing: try the 4-4-6 breathing pattern. Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 6. The longer exhale activates your parasympathetic nervous system -- it physically slows your heart rate. Do this for 5 minutes.

Goodnight. You have done enough today.

## Works with

- **Food Coach** -- when the Food Coach detects emotional eating patterns, it flags the Wellness Guide. Conversely, the Wellness Guide may flag food-related emotional patterns for the Food Coach
- **Seeker** -- the Wellness Guide can ask the Seeker to find past journal entries, daily notes, or previous sessions that provide relevant context
- **Connector** -- recurring emotional themes across the vault may be surfaced by the Connector for the Wellness Guide's awareness

## Tips

- **Come as you are.** You do not need to describe your feelings perfectly. "I feel bad" is a perfectly valid starting point.
- **It is not a replacement for therapy.** The Wellness Guide says this clearly and means it. If you are in active therapy, use this agent to practice between sessions and prepare insights for your next appointment.
- **Ask for specific techniques.** "Guide me through a mindfulness exercise" or "Help me with cognitive restructuring" -- the Wellness Guide adapts to what you need.
- **Use it before bed.** The grounding exercises and brain-dump technique are particularly effective for sleep-disrupting anxiety.
- **Save important insights.** When something resonates, the Wellness Guide will suggest saving it through the Scribe. These notes become a personal library of what works for you.
- **Set up your preferences during onboarding.** Tell the Architect whether you prefer CBT, ACT, Mindfulness, or "surprise me" -- the Wellness Guide will lean into your preferred approach while still drawing from all three when useful.
- **Every session ends with grounding.** No matter what you discussed, the Wellness Guide always brings you back to the present before closing. You will never be left hanging in a difficult emotional state.
